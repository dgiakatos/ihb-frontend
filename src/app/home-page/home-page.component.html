About Us

International Health Booklet (IHB) was developed by 4 students of the Computer Science Department of the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki in 2020. It is a project developed in the context of the course "Web Information Systems" with Athena Vakali being the course coordinator.
IHB is a web application that aims to help the fulfillment of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) set in 2015 by the United Nations General Assembly. These are a collection of 17 global goals designed to be a "blueprint to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all" and are intended to be achieved by the year 2030. IHB has to do with the 4th SDG which is "Good Health and Well-Being".
It is an international health booklet, as its name implies of course. Its purpose is to immediately inform medical organisations and doctors in the case of an emergency about a patients medical history. For example, if somebody travels either abroad or within his country and needs urgent medical help, health organisations and doctors will be able, using our platform, to have access to the patients medical history in order to nurse him more securely as they will have knowledge of the surgeries he has already had, his allergies etc. IHB is aimed to be used by civilians, doctors and health organisations and its administrators.

Hospitals

In today's world at least 400 million people have no basic healthcare, and 40 percent lack social protection. More than 1.6 billion people live in fragile settings where protracted crises, combined with weak national capacity to deliver basic health services, present a significant challenge to global health. In the fight to change this situation, hospitals need to play a major role. Hospitals matter to people â€“ the hospital experience often marking a central point in their lives. They matter to health systems: they are instrumental for care coordination and integration and have a key role to play in supporting other health-care providers (including primary health care) and in community outreach and home-based services. They also often provide a setting for education of doctors, nurses and other health-care professionals and are a critical base for clinical research. They must be resilient and able to maintain and scale up services in emergency situations. Hospitals complement and amplify the effectiveness of many other parts of the health system, providing continuous availability of services for acute and complex conditions. Hospitals concentrate scarce resources within well-planned referral networks to respond efficiently to population health needs. Thus, International Health Booklet, an online system empowering doctors and hospitals around the world, can play a major role in order to acheive good health and well being for people all around the world.
